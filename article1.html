Peanut Butter Waffles

Luke Seeley

Maybe it's just me, or maybe it's just because it reminds me of my dad, but one thing I love to have for breakfast at home is to pop some waffles in the toaster and lather them up with peanut butter and a whole lot of maple syrup. I know it's completley unhealthy, and you will definatly need to brush those teeth after, but Penaut Butter Waffles are the way to go (unless you are going to make breakfast from scratch). This article will provide the reasons why you should make this, and how to make it.

Published: April 9, 2020

[Image Credit: Luke Seeley]

One of my favorite things to have for breakfast, if I'm feeling like being lazy is to have my Peanut Butter Waffles. Since this is not the healthiest choice for breakfast, I typically only have them once a week. Peanut Butter Waffles are simple to make, which is good for someone like me who lacks sufficient cooking skils. I know that some find it a strange choice, I know that my Mom and Step-Mom find it strange, and even gross. I normally don't like my food soggy especially when you expect something to be hard and crunchy, but this breakfast is the kind that mealts in your mouth. My enjoyment of this, is not without bias. It's one of the few things my dad makes as well, and he was the one who taught me the proper way to prepare the waffles.

It is fairly simple to make. All you need are:

  • Freezer Waffles
  • A Toaster
  • A Microwave
  • Peanut Butter
  • Mapple Syrup

Once you have all of that, the process to make the waffles takes about 5 - 10 minutes. The first thing you need to do is put your waffles in the toaster. I usually prefer to have 4 waffles, but if you don't have a double toaster with 4 slots, it will take longer to toast. After toasting, you will swipe peanut butter over the waffles, filling the waffles with peanut butter. Next poor on the maple syrup, enought to have a coat over the whole waffle. The last step is to put the waffles in the microwave for 30 seconds, for 4 waffles, adjust the time as needed based on your microwave and if you are going to have fewer waffles. Microwaving the waffles will allow the maple syrup to sink in throughout the waffles, leaving them nice and moist.
